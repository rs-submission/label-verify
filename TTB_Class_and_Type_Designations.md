# TTB Class and Type Designations — Reference

A consolidated reference to the federal "class and type" (standards of identity)
designations that must appear on U.S. alcohol beverage labels, for **distilled
spirits**, **wine**, and **malt beverages**.

*Compiled from 27 CFR Parts 4, 5, and 7 (as modernized by T.D. TTB–176, eff. 2022,
and later amendments) and TTB's Beverage Alcohol Manuals. This is a working
reference, not legal advice; confirm against the current eCFR and the applicable
BAM before filing a COLA.*

---

## How "class" and "type" work

The class/type designation is one of the mandatory items that must appear in the
**same field of vision** as the brand name and alcohol content (the "brand label").
The framework differs by commodity:

| Commodity | Governing rule | How designations are set |
|---|---|---|
| Distilled spirits | 27 CFR 5.141–5.156 | Closed set of **classes**, each subdivided into defined **types** (standards of identity) |
| Wine | 27 CFR 4.21 | **Nine numbered classes**, each with defined types |
| Malt beverages | 27 CFR 7.141–7.147 | A handful of class designations recognized by **trade understanding**, plus specialty rules |

A product that conforms to no standard of identity is a **specialty product** and is
designated with a distinctive or fanciful name plus a statement of composition.

## 1. Distilled Spirits — 27 CFR 5.142–5.156

Distilled spirits are divided into classes, which are further divided into specific
types. A product conforming to more than one standard may be labeled with any single
class designation to which it conforms.

### Classes

| § | Class | Representative types / notes |
|---|---|---|
| 5.142 | **Neutral spirits or alcohol** | **Vodka** (neutral spirits without distinctive character, aroma, taste, or color); **grain spirits** (neutral spirits from grain, stored in oak). Distilled at or above 95% ABV (190° proof). |
| 5.143 | **Whisky / whiskey** | See whisky type table below. Distilled below 95% ABV from a fermented grain mash, stored in oak, bottled at ≥ 40% ABV. |
| 5.144 | **Gin** | **Distilled gin**, **redistilled gin**, **compounded gin**. ("London dry" etc. are trade terms.) Main flavor must be juniper. |
| 5.145 | **Brandy** | **Fruit brandy** (incl. **grape brandy**; named-fruit brandies such as apple, peach, cherry); **Cognac** (grape brandy, distinctive product of the Cognac region of France); **dried fruit brandy**; **pomace brandy / marc**; **residue brandy**; **neutral brandy**. |
| 5.146 | **Blended applejack** | Apple brandy blended with neutral spirits, within prescribed limits. |
| 5.147 | **Rum** | Class "rum." Geographic distinctive products include **Puerto Rico rum**, **Jamaica rum**, **Demerara rum**. |
| 5.148 | **Agave spirits** | Includes **Tequila** and **Mezcal** (distinctive products of Mexico). |
| 5.150 | **Cordials and liqueurs** | Designated "cordial" or "liqueur"; commonly identified by predominant flavor (e.g., "crème de [flavor]"). Minimum sugar content applies. |
| 5.151 | **Flavored spirits** | Base spirit + predominant flavor, bottled at ≥ 30% ABV — e.g., "Cherry Vanilla Flavored Bourbon Whisky," "Pineapple Flavored Tequila." |
| 5.152 | **Imitations** | Products imitating a standard spirit; must be labeled "imitation." |
| 5.156 | **Distilled spirits specialty products** | Anything not meeting another standard: distinctive/fanciful name + statement of composition, in the same field of vision. |

*(§§ 5.149 and 5.153 are reserved.)*

### Whisky types (27 CFR 5.143)

| Type | Key standard |
|---|---|
| **Bourbon whisky** | ≥ 51% corn; new charred oak. |
| **Rye whisky** | ≥ 51% rye; new charred oak. |
| **Wheat whisky** | ≥ 51% wheat; new charred oak. |
| **Malt whisky** | ≥ 51% malted barley; new charred oak. |
| **Rye malt whisky** | ≥ 51% malted rye; new charred oak. |
| **Corn whisky** | ≥ 80% corn; need not be stored in oak (if stored, used or uncharred). |
| **American single malt whisky** | 100% malted barley, single U.S. distillery; added 2024 (5-year transition). |
| **"Straight"** (modifier) | Any of the above stored ≥ 2 years in the required oak. |
| **Whisky distilled from [grain] mash** | Same mash bills but stored in used or uncharred oak. |
| **Light whisky** | Distilled 80–95% ABV; stored in used/uncharred oak. |
| **Blended whisky** | ≥ 20% straight whisky on a proof-gallon basis, blended with other whisky or neutral spirits. |
| **A blend of straight whiskies** | Mixture of straight whiskies. |
| **Spirit whisky** | Mixture of neutral spirits and ≥ 5% straight whisky / whisky. |
| **Distinctive products** | **Scotch**, **Irish**, **Canadian** whisky (made per the laws of the named country). |

### Common cross-class modifiers

- **Age statement** — required for whisky aged < 4 years (other than corn whisky) and optional/required in other defined cases.
- **"Bottled in Bond" / "Bonded"** — ≥ 100° proof, one distilling season, one distiller, one plant, aged ≥ 4 years in a bonded warehouse.
- **State of distillation** — required for certain "straight" whiskies.

## 2. Wine — 27 CFR 4.21 (Nine Classes)

| Class | Designation | Description / common types |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Grape wine** | Table/"light" wine (7–14% ABV) and dessert wine. For *still* grape wine, a **varietal** (e.g., "Pinot Noir"), semi-generic, or geographic designation may appear in place of the class name. |
| **2** | **Sparkling grape wine** | Effervescent from secondary fermentation in a closed container. Type designations: **Champagne** (bottle-fermented), **crackling / pétillant / frizzante**. |
| **3** | **Carbonated grape wine** | Made effervescent by **injected** CO₂. |
| **4** | **Citrus wine** | From citrus fruit; e.g., citrus table and citrus dessert wine. |
| **5** | **Fruit wine** | From fruit other than grapes (incl. **berry wine**). Apple → **cider**, pear → **perry** (and must be so designated if lacking vinous character). |
| **6** | **Wine from other agricultural products** | E.g., **honey wine / mead**, **raisin wine**, **sake**. Designated "wine" qualified by the product name or a statement of composition. |
| **7** | **Aperitif wine** | ≥ 15% ABV, grape wine with added brandy or alcohol and flavorings; includes **vermouth**. |
| **8** | **Imitation, substandard, or "other than standard" wine** | Must carry the qualifying designation. |
| **9** | **Retsina wine** | Grape table wine flavored with resin. |

**Notes**

- "Table" ("light") and "dessert" wines **need not** be designated as such.
- For Classes 4, 5, and 6, effervescent versions get a further **"sparkling"** (natural secondary fermentation) or **"carbonated"** (injected CO₂) designation — e.g., "Sparkling Peach Wine."
- Appellation of origin becomes mandatory when a vintage date, varietal designation, or term such as "Estate Bottled" is used.

## 3. Malt Beverages — 27 CFR 7.141–7.147

No rigid standards of identity; designation rests on **trade understanding**.

### Class designations (products ≥ 0.5% ABV)

| Designation | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Malt beverage** | The generic class; any malt beverage may use it. |
| **Beer** | Per trade understanding. |
| **Lager / lager beer** | Per trade understanding. |
| **Ale** | Top-fermented; no added coloring/flavoring beyond those recognized. |
| **Porter** | Top-fermented. |
| **Stout** | Top-fermented. |
| **Malt liquor** | Per trade understanding. |

Class designations may be preceded or followed by **color words** (amber, brown, red, golden) and **descriptive terms** (dry, export, cream, pale).

### Type designations and special rules (27 CFR 7.143–7.144)

- **Wheat** — fermentable base ≥ 25% malted wheat, precedes the class (e.g., "Wheat Ale").
- **Rye** — fermentable base ≥ 25% malted rye, precedes the class.
- **Black and tan** — a product blending two classes (e.g., "Black and Tan, Stout and Ale").
- **Beverages with added fruit, honey, spices, etc.** — if the added ingredients are TTB-recognized as traditional, the product may be labeled per trade understanding with an identifying modifier (e.g., "Cherry Ale," "Ale with cherry juice, cinnamon, and nutmeg"). An unmodified designation like "beer" or "ale" alone is **not** acceptable for such products.

### Specialty products (27 CFR 7.147)

A malt beverage not known to the trade under a standard designation — typically due to non-traditional ingredients or processes — must bear a **distinctive or fanciful name plus a statement of composition**, which serves as its class designation.

### Products under 0.5% ABV (27 CFR 7.145)

- Must be designated **"malt beverage," "cereal beverage," or "near beer."**
- May **not** use "beer," "lager," "ale," "porter," "stout," or any designation commonly applied to ≥ 0.5% ABV products.
- If "near beer" is used, both words must match in size, style, color, and background.
